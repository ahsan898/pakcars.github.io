The Lamborghini Sián FKP 37 is a limited-production hybrid supercar that takes inspiration from both classic design and future technology.

Here's a breakdown of its features and specifications:

Powertrain

A naturally-aspirated 6.5L V12 engine producing 785 horsepower (hp) and 720 Nm of torque. An additional electric motor integrated into the gearbox adds 34 hp, bringing the total power output to a staggering 819 hp (or 602 kW). This makes the Sián the most powerful Lamborghini ever produced. The electric motor utilizes supercapacitors for energy storage, a first for a production car. Supercapacitors offer better power delivery compared to traditional batteries, but with shorter range.

Performance

With the combined powertrain, the Sián rockets from 0 to 100 km/h (0-62 mph) in a blistering 2.8 seconds. The electronically limited top speed is an impressive 355 km/h (221 mph).

Design

The Sián's design is based on the Lamborghini Aventador, but with a more aerodynamic and futuristic look. Active aerodynamic elements on the front splitter and rear wing adjust to optimize downforce at different speeds. Lamborghini has incorporated hexagon shapes throughout the design, referencing the company's founding in 1963. Other noteworthy features All-wheel-drive system for enhanced handling and stability. Regenerative braking system to capture energy and recharge the supercapacitors. Lightweight materials used extensively throughout the car to keep weight down.

Limited edition

Only 63 units of the Sián FKP 37 were produced, making it an incredibly exclusive and collectible car.
